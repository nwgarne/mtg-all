# mtg-all.dirtyshoulders.com — Site-wide Design & Frontend QA

_Multi-agent eval: 12 specialist evaluators -> 12 adversarial verifiers -> design-director synthesis, over real screenshots (Chromium + WebKit; phone/desktop/wide), live code, and captured perf. 25 agents, ~2.77M tokens, 2026-06-27._

## Overall: 79/100

This is a genuinely senior-grade, agency-tier build with a world-class engineering substrate (OKLCH "bone-on-ink" tokens, measured AA contrast, viewport virtualization of ~13k cards at 60fps, exemplary CSP/ARIA, near-pixel WebKit parity) and one truly distinctive surface — the search-every-printing overlay + lightbox. But it is not yet "the best-looking MTG site ever made," and the gap is specific and honest: the entire visual identity is inherited wholesale from decks.dirtyshoulders and contains nothing derived from Magic itself (no WUBRG color pie, no mana pip, no set-symbol, no era signature), so it reads as a beautiful generic dark-editorial skin that happens to hold MTG data; the homepage hero has zero card art by construction (no hero__art element at all); the year grid is a uniform muddy tile-wall with no hierarchy or history-of-Magic narrative despite 34 years of gorgeous art and per-year card/set/value data sitting right there; and there is essentially no choreography — the most image-led surfaces pop in with no motion. The verifiers materially corrected the dossier in one big way: the "raw white 404" that six dimensions flagged as P0/P1 is a local python http.server artifact — production Caddy already serves the branded /404.html (caddy.snippet wires handle_errors), so that whole cluster collapses to a single verify-in-prod check, not a defect. Net: an excellent foundation with its signature ambition unspent. Land an MTG-native identity, a committed art-led hero, a year-grid with hierarchy/narrative, and 2-3 motion signatures, and this jumps from solid-79 to a legitimate high-80s/90 showpiece.

## Already world-class (preserve)

- Atmospheric depth system (global.css): 5-layer body gradient of warm radial pools under an SVG film-grain overlay (blend, opacity 0.32) + 3px CRT scanlines gives the dark page a cinematic, expensive 'drafted on graph paper, lit by candlelight' floor most AI sites never achieve. Preserve exactly.
- OKLCH warm-neutral 'bone on ink' palette (tokens.css): blacks read warm-charcoal, text reads cream, never generic #000/#fff. Measured, verifiable contrast (body 14.1:1, accent links 8.7:1, prices 10.4:1, placeholders 4.6:1) that holds identically in Chromium and WebKit.
- Typographic voice: the Antonio condensed-slab + Fraunces italic serif accent + IBM Plex Mono kicker triad, and the hero stroke-glyph + solid + italic lockup ('MTG BY year.'). A real movie-poster/editorial signature, not a stock H1 — and it is ONE family doing the stroke/solid contrast (not the 'colliding families' the type dimension misdiagnosed).
- The search overlay + card lightbox (search-desktop.png, lightbox-desktop.png): a full-bleed dark printings grid headlined by the set NAME (the literal answer to 'which sets was this printed in') with gold price callouts, and a crisp full-res card over a dimmed scrim, kept pristine because content (z3) sits above the grain layers (z1-2). This is the most distinctive, most MTG-feeling surface and the one moment with real wow — protect and amplify it.
- Viewport virtualization (year.js): single-active-controller invariant with rigorous listener teardown, atomic replaceChildren swaps, overflow-anchor:none, measured-rowH snapping, algebraically-verified spacer math — a ~13k-card scope holds ~683 DOM nodes at CLS ~0.011. Best-in-class, and the reason every elevation move is low-risk to add.
- Accessibility engineering (88, top-tier for the category): WAI-ARIA APG combobox to spec, real focus traps + opener restore on every overlay, polite sr-only live regions, plural-aware microcopy, 'No price' chips, 44px touch targets gated on (hover:none)/(pointer:coarse) not just width, display:contents accordion headings, forced-colors hero refill, iOS 16px zoom pin.
- Cross-browser discipline: every backdrop-filter and mask-image carries a -webkit- twin, svh->vh->px viewport-unit ladder, ResizeObserver feature-guarded, and the 641-900px tablet toolbar converts to snap horizontal scrollers so the sticky stack never balloons. The branded /404.html is correctly wired in Caddy (handle_errors -> /404.html) — production users do NOT see the raw error page the local audit captured.
- Layout-shift engineering: fixed-aspect image box (aspect-ratio 488/680), pinned --card-tile-footer for uniform tile height, per-width font-swap reflow reservations, and a dedicated counts backing-plate that holds >=4.5:1 over pale 2025/2026 art. CLS ~0.01 on every page.

## The gap — cross-cutting themes

### It is a beautiful generic skin, not an MTG-derived world
Verified P1 (identity-1): every core stylesheet header literally reads 'decks.dirtyshoulders.com', the body is hard-pinned data-bracket=3, and the gold accent is a competitive-deck BRACKET color, not an MTG choice. Across every home/year shot there is NO WUBRG/mana-color vocabulary, no mana pip, no set symbol, no MTG glyph anywhere. The look could be reskinned for wines or sneakers by swapping the data. This is the single biggest lever to 'best MTG site ever' and nothing in the visual language currently points at Magic — the one obvious place to inject it (the hero) is empty by construction.

### The site never lets MTG art — its best asset — sing
The homepage hero has zero card art (verified: no hero__art element exists in index.html, so all the hero.css masking/opacity machinery is dead there); the #all/search grids upscale the 146px Scryfall 'small' image ~1.8x into ~260px tiles so the densest views look soft; and every year/picker tile triple-scrims its art to dim brown texture. 34 years of gorgeous splash art is muted everywhere it appears. The lightbox proves full-fidelity art looks stunning here — that confidence reaches almost nothing else.

### The homepage is a catalog, not a designed archive with a story
The year grid is a uniform 4-up wall of equal-weight tiles (verified P1/P2): no featured year, no scale variation, no era hierarchy, and — despite cards/sets/value-per-year sitting in years.json — no visualization of Magic exploding from 1,596 cards in 1993 to ~13,000/year. The grid also orphans its final row to two lonely tiles on every desktop/wide width (34/4), a flaw that recurs in the year picker (same .year-grid 4-col lock). The most ownable, uniquely-MTG signature available is left on the table.

### Resting micro-interaction craft is real; choreography is absent
Hover/focus/reduced-motion are genuinely well-built, but there are only two bespoke keyframes in the whole codebase, the reveal fires on just 3 static hero nodes and never on any JS-rendered content (year/all heroes pop in too), there is no IntersectionObserver, the search overlay opens with a hard cut, exits never animate, and a declared 5-step duration scale plays at one speed (89% --dur-fast). For the stated bar, motion is the gap. The back-to-top spring proves the team CAN do JS-driven state motion — they did it exactly once.

### Inherited dead scaffolding is the structural tell of a hand-me-down
Verified across dimensions: deck-card/deck-index/sword-mark, the entire is-gc/is-in/is-out/swap-from card-state system (the status palette some dimensions praised as a STRENGTH is 100% unreachable), mako/rust ramps, b2/b4 bracket re-skins, FLAT_CAP=200 with contradictory 'top 200' comments, a duplicate --topbar-stuck-offset, and several stale comments all ship unused. Invisible to users but it means the 'design system' the team reasons about is mostly irrelevant to this product, inviting future inconsistency and making the craft feel borrowed rather than purpose-built.

### The audit was shot against a dev server, so several 'live' claims are unverified
The headline 404 finding (flagged P0-P1 in six dimensions) is the local python http.server default; production Caddy already serves the branded /404.html. The perf.json capture finished before cross-origin images loaded (img:0 on both pages), so the '168KB fast homepage' excludes ~34 cover fetches and the lazy-load 'proof' is actually the capture being blind to image bytes. Re-measure against the real Caddy host with network-idle + throttled mobile before treating either as fact — the true gaps are the 5MB monolithic year JSON and the soft grid images, not the 404.

## Signature moves (path to best-MTG-site-ever)

### 1. The Ridgeline — a scrollable data-viz of all 34 years of Magic
**Vision:** Between the hero and the year grid, a single wide inline-SVG ridgeline/area chart of cards-printed-per-year from 1993 to 2026 (the data already lives in years.json). Each ridge is tinted by era — early gold, modern rust, Universes Beyond cooler — and the line draws itself in on scroll-into-view via stroke-dashoffset. Hovering a peak surfaces that year's stats (N cards / N sets / $value) and lifts the corresponding tile in the grid below; clicking jumps to /<year>/. The silent y-axis tells the story of Magic exploding from 1,596 cards to ~13,000 a year. Pure static SVG, no library, fully CSP-safe.

**Why:** It is the single most ownable, uniquely-MTG signature available, and it transforms the homepage from a catalog into a narrative about the history of the game. It directly leverages the one asset that should make a by-year MTG site gorgeous (34 years of data), reframes the otherwise-uniform grid as the index TO a story, and is exactly the kind of move an Awwwards juror stops scrolling for and fans screenshot. Verified feasible: data is present, the build already does CSP-safe DOM construction, and the prefers-reduced-motion guard exists to gate the draw-in.

### 2. Living card wall — an art-led, foil-lit hero that finally lets Magic sing
**Vision:** Replace today's empty hero (the homepage has NO art element at all) with a deliberate device: a slow-drifting mosaic/parallax of ~12-20 iconic card arts at ~0.45-0.6 opacity, the 'MTG by year' wordmark knocked OUT of the imagery via mask/blend, scaled up. Optionally one foreground hero card that holographically tilts and throws a moving specular highlight on pointer-move — the iconic foil-card feel. The 115,805 / 34 / 1,000+ figures surface as a confident stat-row. On year pages, wire the existing fed-nothing hero__art div so '1993 EVERY card.' bleeds Black Lotus and '2026' bleeds its hero. Freezes to a composed still under reduced-motion.

**Why:** It closes the two biggest gaps in one move — the generic, art-free hero and the universally-muted MTG art — and sets the premium tone for everything below. The lightbox already proves full-fidelity card art looks stunning here; this extends that confidence to the most-viewed screen. Cleaner than the dossier implied because there is no 0.16 art layer to fight: the homepage hero is currently empty by construction, so this is pure addition.

### 3. Holo-foil card interaction on tiles + lightbox
**Vision:** Make the card the hero interaction it deserves. On the #all grid tiles and especially in the lightbox, add a pointer-driven 3D tilt (rotateX/rotateY off cursor position) plus a sweeping rainbow specular gradient that tracks the pointer — the classic 'foil card catching the light' — gated to rare/mythic for restraint and meaning. In the lightbox this becomes the centerpiece: a single full-res card you tilt and catch the light on. Transform + one gradient layer, GPU-friendly, already has a reduced-motion escape hatch.

**Why:** Foil-tilt is the one micro-interaction every MTG player instantly recognizes and loves, and it converts the strongest-but-static moment (the lightbox) and the densest screen (#all) into something tactile and unmistakably Magic. It is exactly the 'craft' Awwwards jurors reward, and it plugs into the existing easing tokens and virtualization with near-zero risk — the back-to-top spring already proves the team can do GPU-driven state motion.

### 4. A WUBRG color-pie spine that makes the brand unmistakably Magic
**Vision:** Introduce the five mana colors in OKLCH (in-key with the warm neutrals) and thread them through load-bearing surfaces: a five-color spectrum hairline under the hero rule, color-of-the-year tinting on each year tile's number-glow and counts separator, mana-pip section markers replacing the generic '// the archive' mono kicker, and per-type color/icon on the #all type pills. Gold stays the chrome/value/CTA accent; WUBRG carries the soul. Repurpose the dead mako/rust ramps as two of the five, and pull the card-frame language from the lightbox forward into the tiles so the whole site reads as Magic, not as a deck-site reskin.

**Why:** Right now the entire identity is inherited from decks.dirtyshoulders with nothing derived from Magic — no mana pip, no set symbol, no color pie anywhere. This is the verified #1 blocker to 'best MTG site ever.' A disciplined WUBRG system is the move that turns premium-generic into premium-MTG, and it simultaneously retires the inherited dead ramps so the system reads as purpose-built end to end.

### 5. Era-banded grid with a rotating spotlight year
**Vision:** Break the uniform 34-tile checkerboard into authored rhythm: group years into named eras with slim mono dividers ('1993-2002 The Early Era', 'Modern', 'Universes Beyond'), promote the current year (or a daily-rotating spotlight) to a 2x2 hero tile with its art at full strength, let milestone years (Alpha '93, anniversary years) span 2 columns, and close the grid with a deliberate end-cap that eliminates the orphaned last row by design. Stagger the tiles' entrance with the same IntersectionObserver reveal as the ridgeline.

**Why:** It replaces the 'safe/templated' even grid — the read that most undercuts the premium positioning the rest of the system earns — with editorial hierarchy and a clear focal point, reinforces the Ridgeline's history narrative as chapters, and fixes the orphan-row composition flaw on both the homepage and the year picker (shared .year-grid rule) in one stroke.

# Appendix — per-dimension detail (verified findings only)

## Visual identity & aesthetic impact — 71/100

_Solid, professional, cohesive — but not yet "best-looking MTG site ever." The atmospheric system (5-layer body gradient + grain + scanlines, OKLCH warm-neutral "bone on ink" palette, Antonio/Fraunces/IBM Plex Mono triad) is genuinely agency-grade and the year-card full-bleed-art tiles are a strong MTG-native idea. Two things cap the score. First, the entire identity is inherited wholesale from decks.dirtyshoulders (tokens.css/global.css/hero.css headers literally read "decks.dirtyshoulders.com", body is hard-pinned data-bracket="3", the gold accent is a deck-BRACKET color, and dead SaaS scaffolding like .deck-card, .sword-mark, bracket B2/B3/B4 re-skins ship unused) — so nothing in the visual language is derived from Magic itself: no WUBRG mana identity, no MTG iconography, no era signature. It is a beautiful generic dark-editorial skin that happens to hold MTG data, not a distinctively-MTG world. Second, the homepage full view collapses into a near-uniform wall of dark tiles (older-year art crops are muddy and interchangeable) and the hero under-commits (small wordmark on near-empty space, hero art at 0.16-0.18 opacity is effectively invisible).
